## 🧪 Scenario 1: Social Impact — Meal Swipe Sharing

**Category:** Social Good / Campus Life  
**Complexity:** Medium

```
I want to build an app where college students can share their leftover meal plan 
swipes with students who can't afford food. Think of it like Uber, but for 
unused meal swipes. Students with extra swipes can "donate" or sell them at a 
discount, and students who need meals can claim them. It would work with the 
campus dining system.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should identify food insecurity statistics
- Shark Tank investors should ask about campus dining partnerships
- Pitch deck should highlight social impact angle

## 🧪 Scenario 2: EdTech — AI Study Buddy

**Category:** Education Technology  
**Complexity:** High

```
An AI-powered study buddy app that reads your class notes, lecture slides, and 
textbook PDFs, then generates practice questions, flashcards, and mini-quizzes 
personalized to your weak areas. It tracks what you get wrong and focuses on 
those topics. Students can also share flashcard decks with classmates.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should reference EdTech market size
- Shark Tank should question AI accuracy and differentiation from Quizlet
- Pitch deck should have clear feature comparison

---

## 🧪 Scenario 3: Marketplace — Peer-to-Peer Textbook Rental

**Category:** E-commerce / Marketplace  
**Complexity:** Medium

```
A platform where students can rent textbooks from each other instead of buying 
new. Like Airbnb but for college textbooks. Students list their books after the 
semester, set a rental price, and other students can rent for the next semester. 
Built-in payments, reviews, and condition tracking. We take a 10% transaction fee.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should compare to Chegg, Amazon Rental, campus bookstores
- Shark Tank should probe about logistics and book condition disputes
- Pitch deck should model unit economics with 10% take rate

---

## 🧪 Scenario 4: Local Commerce — Food Truck Tracker

**Category:** Lifestyle / Local Business  
**Complexity:** Low-Medium

```
A food truck tracking app for college towns. Students get real-time GPS locations 
of food trucks near campus, can see menus, wait times, and ratings. They can 
pre-order for pickup to skip the line. Food trucks get a free marketing channel 
and pay us for premium features like push notifications to nearby students.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should rate this as a local/niche market
- Shark Tank may question scalability beyond one campus
- Pitch deck should focus on go-to-market strategy

## 🧪 Scenario 5: Vague Idea (Tests Clarification)

**Category:** Undefined  
**Complexity:** Intentionally vague

```
I want to do something with AI and fitness. Maybe like a personal trainer app 
or something. I work out a lot and I feel like there's something there.
```

**What to watch for:**
- The orchestrator should **ask clarifying questions** before proceeding
- Should ask about: target user, specific problem, how it makes money
- Should NOT jump straight into market research with vague input

---

## 🧪 Scenario 6: Hardware + Software — Smart Parking

**Category:** IoT / Campus Infrastructure  
**Complexity:** High

```
A smart campus parking system. We install cheap sensors in parking spots that 
detect if a car is parked there. Students open our app to see a real-time map 
of available spots. No more driving around the parking lot for 20 minutes! We 
charge the university a monthly subscription for the sensor network and the app 
is free for students.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should flag hardware costs and installation challenges
- Shark Tank investors should ask about sensor costs and university procurement
- Pitch deck should address the B2B (university) sales cycle

---

## 🧪 Scenario 7: Creator Economy — Campus Influencer Platform

**Category:** Social Media / Creator Economy  
**Complexity:** Medium

```
A platform that connects local businesses near campus with student micro-influencers. 
Students with 500+ followers on Instagram/TikTok sign up, local restaurants and 
shops post campaign briefs (like "post a reel about our new coffee"), students 
create content and get paid $20-50 per post. We take 20% commission from the 
business side.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Market agent should reference the creator economy ($100B+)
- Shark Tank should question quality control and brand safety
- Pitch deck should showcase the two-sided marketplace model
